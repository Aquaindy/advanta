Command Center

Your home screen and launchpad. It tells you what to do next, shows your two readiness scores once your Growth DNA exists, and confirms the platform is healthy — then sends you into the module where the real numbers live.

Onboarding gate Growth DNA snapshot System health No vanity metrics

Why the dashboard looks calm, not crowded

AdGenieHQ never shows fake numbers. So the Command Center deliberately doesn't fill itself with spend/ROAS/CPA tiles before you've connected anything — those live inside each module and in Reports, where they're backed by real data. The home screen's job is to orient you and point to the next action.

1What you'll see — route /

Top to bottom, the Command Center renders:

  1. Header — "Your AI Growth Operating System" with a one-line description of how the agents work under approval.
  2. Onboarding gate — a card that adapts to your progress (see §2). It's the single thing to act on first.
  3. Growth DNA snapshot — appears once your profile exists: Funnel readiness and Paid ads readiness (each /100), with a "View full profile →" link.
  4. System health — three live status cards: API, PostgreSQL, and Redis, each showing Healthy / Degraded / Unreachable.

3Reading the readiness scores

Once generated, the snapshot shows two deterministic scores derived purely from your onboarding answers — not from connected ad data:

  • Funnel readiness — audience clarity, offer clarity, landing pages, brand voice, analytics.
  • Paid ads readiness — budget, conversion goal, audience, geo, platforms, analytics.

They're color-coded: green ≥ 80, grape 50–79, amber below 50 — a quick read on where your setup is strong or thin before you spend.

?FAQ & tips

Why don't I see spend / ROAS / leads on the home screen?

By design — those would be fabricated for a workspace with nothing connected. Connect an integration and run an agent, and the real figures appear in the relevant module and in Reports.

What should I do first?

Follow the onboarding gate: complete onboarding → generate your Growth DNA. That unlocks readiness scores and gives every agent the context it needs.

A health card is red — what now?

It points to a specific dependency (API/Postgres/Redis). If it persists, it's an operational issue for your admin/support, not something in your workspace data.
