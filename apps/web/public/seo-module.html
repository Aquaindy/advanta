SEO & GEO Module

⚡ 60-second quickstart

SEO & GEO → Run SEO audit (crawls + checks your pages) → connect Search Console in Settings → Sync Search Console for keyword opportunities → fix the flagged issues → draft content in Content/Blog → chase links in Outreach.

1Technical SEO audit — /seo · "Run SEO audit"

The seo_audit agent discovers your sitemap (via robots.txt / sitemap.xml / sitemap_index.xml), crawls a handful of real pages, and runs on-page checks. Results appear in the Site overview card with issue counts and saved recommendations.

What it checks per pageFlagged when…
Title tagmissing high
Meta descriptionmissing medium
H1 / headingsmissing or multiple medium
Canonical linkabsent low
Structured data (JSON-LD)none present medium
Open Graph tagsmissing low
FAQ schemanot detected low

The overview also tells you whether a sitemap was found, how many URLs it lists, how many pages were crawled, and which Search Console site is linked.

2Search Console opportunities — "Sync Search Console"

Connect Google Search Console (Settings → Integrations, read-only OAuth), then click Sync Search Console. AdGenieHQ pulls the last 28 days of query data and builds a keyword opportunities table:

  • Query · impressions · clicks · CTR · average position · the top page that ranks for it
  • An opportunity score (0–100) per keyword — high impressions + middling position + low CTR float to the top, so you see the "almost there" wins first.

Search Console must be connected first

No GSC connection = an empty table with a connect prompt (linking to Integrations). The data only ever reflects keywords your site already appears for — nothing is estimated or scraped from third-party tools.

3GEO foundations — Generative Engine Optimization

"GEO" here means making your pages legible to AI answer engines and rich results. The audit specifically flags the structured-data building blocks those engines rely on, as dedicated recommendations:

  • Structured data (JSON-LD) missing — so engines can parse what your page is about
  • FAQ schema missing — the format most likely to be lifted into answers
  • Open Graph missing — clean titles/descriptions when your page is cited or shared

Honest scope

GEO here is about fixing the on-page foundations AI engines need — it checks your markup. It does not (yet) query ChatGPT/Perplexity/Google AI to measure your live answer-engine share of voice.

4Content drafter — /content

The content_writer agent turns a brief into a saved draft awaiting review.

  1. Pick a type — blog post, landing page, ad copy, meta description, email, or social post.
  2. Add the brief — topic (required), optional audience and comma-separated keywords.
  3. Generate draft — uses your configured LLM, or a deterministic template built from your onboarding profile if none.
  4. Review & move it — edit the title/body/SEO fields, then Approve → Publish → Archive (or Reject with a reason). Every step is audit-trailed.

5Blog editor & publishing — /blog/posts

  • Markdown editor with live preview, drag-and-drop image upload, and a cover image picker.
  • AI Assist — outline, expand, refine, suggest a title, suggest meta — applied to the whole draft or a selection.
  • Settings tab — slug (unique per workspace), excerpt, keywords, and SEO meta (title/description).
  • Publish — approved + published posts render on your public blog at /blog and /blog/{slug}; an optional publish webhook can notify an external CMS.

6Backlink outreach — /outreach

Track the sites you want links from and run safe, human-approved outreach.

  1. Add prospects — by domain (with optional contact, relevance score, domain authority), bulk-import a list, or discover them by crawling a competitor's outbound links.
  2. Draft an email — generate a personalized pitch (optionally with an angle + sender name); it lands in draft.
  3. Approve & send — edit, then an Admin approves and sends. SMTP is the only path that actually reaches the recipient.
  4. Track replies — inbound replies are matched back to the thread automatically; mark won/replied and record the live backlink URL. Draft threaded follow-ups as needed.

Emails never auto-send

Drafted outreach always waits for explicit Admin approval, every send is audit-logged with IP + user agent, and AdGenieHQ adds an unsubscribe path. No spam blasts, no surprise sends.

8What's not included (yet)

So you know exactly where the lines are:

  • No third-party keyword databases (Semrush / Ahrefs / DataForSEO) — opportunities come from your Search Console only.
  • No automatic search-intent classification or content-gap mapping.
  • No single "site visibility" index — scoring is per-keyword opportunity.
  • Page-speed scoring lives in the Website module, not here.
  • Domain authority / relevance in Outreach are entered by you, not auto-fetched.

?FAQ & tips

The keyword table is empty.

Connect Google Search Console in Settings → Integrations, then click Sync Search Console. The table fills with the queries your site already ranks for.

How many pages does the audit crawl?

A focused sample (homepage + a few sitemap URLs) so it's fast and respectful of your server — enough to surface systemic issues across templates.

Does the audit need an OpenAI key?

No. The crawl, checks, and Search Console scoring are deterministic. The content drafter and AI Assist use your LLM when configured, with template fallbacks.

Where do published blog posts appear?

On your public blog (/blog and /blog/{slug}) once a post is approved and published with a slug.
