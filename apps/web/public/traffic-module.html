Traffic Module — "Traffic Genie"

A planning & tracking command center for every way you can drive traffic: 32 sources across 10 categories, AI that recommends where to start, generators that write the assets, and a dashboard that scores real results.

Read this first — why Traffic sources are NOT on the Integrations page

Most traffic sources (solo ads, paid email, forums, communities, organic social…) have no OAuth API — you can't "connect" them. So Traffic Genie treats them as a plan & track layer: you create campaigns, generate the assets, run the traffic on the real platform, then log results so the analytics and Quality Guard work off your real numbers. The Integrations page stays reserved for genuine OAuth platforms.

2AI recommendation — /traffic/recommendation

Not sure where to start? Tell the recommender your business type, monthly budget, preference (paid / organic / hybrid), and goal. It scores every source against your inputs and returns:

  • A primary source + why it fits
  • Supporting sources for a balanced mix
  • A 7-day launch plan you can act on immediately

3Traffic campaigns + asset generation — /traffic/campaigns

  1. Create a campaign — pick a source, name it, add the offer, audience, and budget.
  2. Generate assets — one AI generator writes the right assets for that source: ad angles, headlines, primary text, CTAs, video hooks/scripts, SEO clusters, email swipes, pin titles… whatever the source supports.
  3. Review & edit — every asset is saved to the campaign, traceable to the agent run that produced it.
  4. Map a follow-up — connect the campaign to an Omnisend journey (see §5).

Asset generation works with no LLM key too (deterministic fallback), so you always get usable drafts.

4Solo Ads & Quality Guard — /traffic/solo-ads

A dedicated workspace for buying email traffic, with three tabs:

Orders & ROI

Log each order's real numbers (clicks delivered, unique clicks, cost, opt-ins, sales, revenue). AdGenieHQ derives the economics for you:

  • CPC (cost per click), CPL (cost per lead), EPC (earnings per click)
  • ROI / ROAS, opt-in rate

Quality Guard

Click Quality score on an order to get a deterministic 0–100 score, a verdict, and flags (e.g. low unique-click ratio, weak opt-in rate, no sales). A vendor's rolling quality is the average of its scored orders — so good and bad vendors separate themselves over time.

Vendors & Playbook

  • Vendors — track each seller, niche, average CPC, and rolling quality score.
  • Playbook — the Solo Ads agent gives 10 subject lines, 3 email swipes, a 10-point vendor-screening checklist, follow-up, tracking plan, and compliance notes.

Honest by design

Scores and economics come only from numbers you enter — nothing is fabricated. The playbook never implies solo ads guarantee sales.

5Omnisend journeys — /traffic/omnisend

Turn raw traffic into nurtured leads. Pick from 13 core journey types (welcome, solo-ads nurture, abandoned-cart, webinar, re-engagement…) and the agent generates a full automation blueprint: trigger, segmentation, an email + SMS sequence with delays/subjects/body/CTA, exit conditions, and a conversion goal.

  1. Generate a journey blueprint for your offer.
  2. Map a campaign — stores a "Source – Vendor – Campaign" segment + tag on the traffic campaign.
  3. Tag opt-ins — sync leads into Omnisend with that tag (real contact tagging), which triggers the automation you built.

API boundary (important)

Omnisend's API is tag-based — it can't create automations or segments for you. So journeys are blueprints you build once in Omnisend; AdGenieHQ then tags the right contacts to trigger them. You'll see this clearly in the UI.

6UTM builder — /traffic/utm-builder

  • Build clean, consistent tracking links: destination + source / medium / campaign / content / term.
  • Values are normalized automatically (lowercased, spaces → underscores) and your original query string is preserved.
  • Every link is saved so your team reuses the same conventions — no more messy, inconsistent UTMs.

7Traffic dashboard & optimizer — /traffic/dashboard

Where all your real results come together. Until you log data, it stays empty (by design — no fake metrics).

  1. Log results — record per-source, per-day numbers (clicks, leads, sales, cost, revenue). Solo Ads orders feed in automatically.
  2. Read the rollups — totals plus per-source and per-campaign economics (CPC / CPL / CPS / ROAS / EPC / conversion), with a paid vs paid-email vs organic split.
  3. Traffic quality score — each source gets a 0–100 score so winners and losers are obvious; profitable sources sort first.
  4. Optimize — the Traffic Optimizer agent surfaces prioritized next-best-actions (Scale / Fix-or-pause / Improve / Double-down) and saves them as recommendations with an AI narrative.

?FAQ & tips

The Traffic pages look empty — is something broken?

No. There's no fake data anywhere in AdGenieHQ. Campaigns, Solo Ads orders, and the dashboard fill in only once you create/log real records.

What's the difference between a Traffic campaign and an Ads campaign?

An Ads campaign is synced live from Google/Meta/LinkedIn via API. A Traffic campaign is your plan + generated assets + logged results for any of the 32 sources — including channels with no API.

How do solo ads connect to email follow-up?

Log the order in Solo Ads → map the campaign to an Omnisend journey → tag the opt-ins. The tag triggers the automation you built once in Omnisend.

Do I need a paid LLM key?

No — recommendation, asset generation, journeys, and the optimizer all have deterministic fallbacks. A key just makes the copy richer.
